Microbiome Interaction Network Database

A compact Django-based database for curated microbiome study data, centered on study groups, explicit comparisons, qualitative findings, and quantitative taxon values.

The project centralizes evidence from published studies so any team can review papers, inspect cohorts and arms, compare disease and control groups, and trace taxon-level findings in ways that go beyond spreadsheets alone.

What’s in the database

The database brings together paper details, study groups, comparisons, organisms, qualitative findings, quantitative values, and extra metadata in one place.

Why it matters

Microbiome results are often reported as changes between groups rather than clean tables. Organizing them this way makes the literature easier to compare and reuse.

How data was curated

Each paper is reviewed by hand, organized into groups and comparisons, and checked before import so the original context is not lost.
